The subordinating conjunction dass. (From Chapter 11)

Clauses joined by subordinating conjunctions can follow these two word order patterns:

  1. The sentence begins with the main clause: In this case, the main clause has regular word order (meaning verb is second), and the dependent clause created by the conjunction has dependent word order (with verb last):
    Clause 1 Conj. Clause 2
    Ich muss weit fahren, weil die Polizei jagt mir sind.
    (I must drive far because the police are chasing me.)
  2. The sentence begins with the dependent clause: In this case the verb-second rule applies, moving the subject of the main clause after the verb:
    Clause 1 Clause 2
    Weil die Polizei Ich muss weit fahren, die Polizei jagt mir sind.
